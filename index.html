EE-REID: A Tutorial on Easy and Efficient Re-identification

Proposed by: Lamarr Institute for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence, Germany

Contact: simon.kluettermann@tu-dortmund.de

Date: WACV 2025, Half-day Event

Abstract

We propose a tutorial for the IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision (WACV) 2025, presenting our Python library EE-REID. The library enables novice researchers to easily perform re-identification experiments. The tutorial will be structured as a half-day event, featuring hands-on tasks using Google Colab notebooks. Attendees will work on three training tasks of increasing complexity, assisted by the presenters.

Course Description

Re-identification is a growing field in machine learning. Our library, EE-REID, simplifies the setup of re-identification pipelines, making it accessible even to non-experts. The tutorial will introduce the library, guide participants through training tasks, and cover various re-identification challenges, including Graph Neural Networks and Novelty Detection.

Target Audience

This tutorial is intended for graduate students, young researchers, and computer scientists with an interest in computer vision and machine learning. We anticipate 20 to 40 attendees, but we can scale it accordingly.
